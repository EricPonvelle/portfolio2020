WHO ARE YOU?

My name is Eric Ponvelle, and I am a Technical Communicator based in Atlanta, Georgia.

I worked as a Technical Writer/Instructional Designer in the engineering and software development industries for the past eight years. Through my experience, I have cultivated skills to break down ideas, understand these ideas from others' perspective, and then translate my understanding into concise, accurate text, videos, and websites. Whether I am teaching friends a new board game about an alt-history space race, documenting the set-up process for a mobile terminal, or documenting and testing the process to match and merge duplicate records, I approach learning without fear or hesitation. As a writer, my driver is to create a shared vocabulary for users to learn, regardless of experience.

For more information on my background, please view my resume or my LinkedIn Profile.

BUT, WHAT IS THIS?

Since adolescence, technology instantly captivated me. After a short pursuit of formal education in software development, I found my talent at quickly understanding problems, breaking them down, and reassembling these ideas into more digestible forms coalesced with my drive to inform and share knowledge.

I designed this portfolio to serve two primary purposes: demonstrate my ability to educate while also displaying new ways to inform users.

To this end, this site has a few paths. The demo projects that I wanted to highlight are below. I tried to present each idea--API, release notes, and a user guide--in two formats: a classic, text-and-image-based article as well as a more visually appealing manner, leveraging GIFs, videos, and other neat ideas I enjoyed testing. Above, there's another link to the project showcase, as well as a link to my GitHub account.

Finally, for any of my prior work, please contact me via e-mail or LinkedIn.

ENJOY.
